- Decision: ITERATE
- Justification: The current research plan is scientifically sound but suffers from a critical "Inference Logic Flaw" regarding the marginalization strategy. The current "mean NLL over prior" approach effectively acts as a marginal likelihood, which is less sensitive to structural anomalies than a "Maximum Likelihood" approach. By switching to $\text{Score}(x) = -\log \max_{\theta \in \Theta} p(x|\theta)$, we align the method with the core objective of OoD detection: identifying samples that cannot be explained by *any* valid InD configuration. Furthermore, the proposed refinement of the validation strategy (testing against extreme nuisance parameters rather than a Gaussian blur) is essential to ensure the model is truly robust to nuisance-driven variance, which is a primary requirement of the challenge. These changes directly address the "Structural Robustness" and "Reviewer Confidence" criteria, moving the paper from a potentially flawed implementation to a rigorous, physics-informed study that provides both high performance and actionable scientific insights into which physical scales drive the OoD signal. The ROI is high because these changes are algorithmic refinements that do not require a complete overhaul of the existing WST+NSF pipeline.
