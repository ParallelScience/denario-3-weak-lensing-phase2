**Title: Variational Conditional Scattering-Flow (VCSF) for Robust Baryonic OoD Detection**

To detect subtle inter-code hydrodynamical differences while marginalizing over nuisance parameters, we implement a two-stage pipeline: a Wavelet Scattering Transform (WST) feature extractor followed by a Conditional Normalizing Flow (CNF). We utilize `kymatio` to compute scattering coefficients (scales $1–10$ arcmin) which capture non-Gaussian baryonic signatures while suppressing Gaussian noise. The CNF, implemented as a Masked Autoregressive Flow, is trained on the full 25,856 training maps to learn the conditional density $p(\text{features} | \Omega_m, S_8, T_{AGN}, f_0, \Delta z)$. To meet the 5-minute inference limit, we replace expensive Monte Carlo integration with a lightweight ensemble approach: a pre-trained ResNet-18 regressor predicts the posterior distribution of the five parameters, providing a mean vector $\hat{\theta}$ and an uncertainty covariance $\Sigma$. The final OoD score is the negative log-likelihood $-\log p(\text{features} | \hat{\theta})$, averaged over an ensemble of 5 samples drawn from the regressor's predictive posterior to ensure robustness against nuisance-driven estimation noise. This approach effectively isolates the non-Gaussian structural anomalies caused by hydro-code implementation differences while remaining invariant to the known variance of cosmological and baryonic nuisance parameters.
