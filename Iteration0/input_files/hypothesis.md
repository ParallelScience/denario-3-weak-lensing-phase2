**Title: Contrastive Latent-Space Alignment for Nuisance-Invariant OoD Detection**

The current VCSF approach relies on explicit parameter estimation ($\hat{\theta}$) to condition the flow, which is limited by the high RMSE of nuisance parameter regression. We hypothesize that explicitly forcing the latent representation to be invariant to nuisance parameters via a **Contrastive Adversarial Training** objective will outperform conditional density estimation. 

Instead of predicting $\hat{\theta}$, we will train a feature extractor (using the existing WST coefficients) to map maps into a latent space $z$ where the distribution is independent of the nuisance parameters $\{T_{AGN}, f_0, \Delta z\}$ but sensitive to the underlying hydro-code physics. We will implement a "Gradient Reversal Layer" (GRL) attached to a nuisance-classifier head, forcing the feature extractor to minimize the classifier's ability to identify the nuisance parameters. Simultaneously, we will use a **Contrastive Loss** (e.g., SupCon) to cluster samples with identical cosmological parameters ($\Omega_m, S_8$) together, regardless of their nuisance realizations. 

The final OoD score will be the **Mahalanobis distance** of the latent vector $z$ from the InD manifold (modeled as a Gaussian Mixture Model in the latent space). This approach bypasses the noisy regression of nuisance parameters and directly optimizes for a representation that is robust to known nuisances while maximizing sensitivity to the structural anomalies inherent in the OoD hydro-code implementation. This should reduce the false-positive rate in the low-FPR regime by ensuring that the "nuisance-induced" variance is collapsed in the latent space, leaving only the "hydro-code-induced" variance as the primary signal for OoD detection.
