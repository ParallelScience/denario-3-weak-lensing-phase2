The VCSF pipeline is conceptually sound, but the current results reveal a critical methodological contradiction that must be addressed to improve performance.

**1. The "Random Chance" Fallacy:**
You report a partial AUC of 0.0590 on your "extreme nuisance" validation set and interpret this as a "profound success" because it indicates the model is "blind" to nuisance parameters. This is a dangerous misinterpretation. In the context of the competition metric (mean TPR over FPR [0.001, 0.05]), a score of 0.05 is the baseline for a random classifier. If your model cannot distinguish between extreme nuisance samples and normal samples, it means your model has **zero sensitivity** to the very parameters it is supposed to be invariant to. While invariance is the goal, you have achieved it by effectively "zeroing out" the signal. If the OoD signal (the hydro-code difference) shares any structural overlap with the nuisance-modulated features, your current VIB/adversarial bottleneck is likely suppressing the actual OoD signal as well.

**2. Over-reliance on Residuals:**
You regressed out the physical parameters from the WST coefficients to create "residuals." This is a high-risk step. If the hydro-code difference manifests as a *change in the relationship* between $\Omega_m/S_8$ and the small-scale structure (e.g., the hydro-code changes how baryonic feedback responds to a given cosmology), your linear regression has effectively destroyed the signal you are trying to detect. You are assuming the OoD signal is purely additive and orthogonal to the cosmological parameters. This is physically unlikely.

**3. Actionable Improvements:**
*   **Abandon the Residual Projection:** Instead of regressing out the parameters, feed the raw WST coefficients *and* the physical parameters ($\Omega_m, S_8, T_{AGN}, f_0, \Delta z$) directly into the Conditional Normalizing Flow. Let the flow learn the conditional density $p(WST | \theta)$. A well-trained CNF is mathematically designed to handle conditional dependencies; by manually "projecting out" features, you are introducing inductive bias that likely discards non-linear interactions between cosmology and hydro-physics.
*   **Re-evaluate the VIB:** The VIB is likely too aggressive. If you must use it, reduce the adversarial weight. The goal is to be invariant to nuisance parameters *while retaining* the sensitivity to the hydro-code difference. Your current results suggest you have achieved invariance at the cost of discriminative power.
*   **Calibration of Errorbars:** You are using the variance of the Random Forest regressor as your `errorbars`. This is a measure of *parameter estimation uncertainty*, not *OoD detection uncertainty*. If the regressor is confident but wrong (or if the map is truly OoD), this errorbar will be misleadingly small. Use the variance of the NLL across the ensemble of the CNF (or multiple forward passes with dropout) as your uncertainty estimate; this better captures the model's "surprise" at the input.
*   **Focus on High-L Sensitivity:** The WST $J=4$ is good, but ensure your coefficients are not dominated by the Gaussian part of the field. If the OoD signal is truly at small scales, verify that your WST configuration is not being washed out by the noise floor. Consider a weighted loss function for the CNF that prioritizes the high-frequency scattering coefficients.

**Summary:** You have built a robust "nuisance-blind" model, but you have likely blinded it to the OoD signal as well. Stop projecting out the physical parameters; let the Conditional Normalizing Flow handle the conditioning natively.
