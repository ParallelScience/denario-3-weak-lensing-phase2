- Decision: ITERATE
- Justification: The current pipeline has established a strong foundation (WST feature extraction + MAF density estimation), but the validation strategy is currently decoupled from the actual challenge objective (hydro-code mismatch). The "synthetic proxy" (Gaussian blur) is indeed a straw man that risks overfitting to resolution loss rather than baryonic feedback physics. To move from "Borderline/Promising" to "Strong Accept/Top-Tier," the following structural improvements are necessary: 
1. **Regressor-Conditioned Inference**: Re-integrating the regressor to provide a MAP estimate for $\theta$ is critical. Averaging over the prior (as currently done) is statistically "noisy" and dilutes the sensitivity to the specific hydro-code signal. 
2. **Sensitivity-Weighted NLL**: The 417 WST coefficients contain varying levels of nuisance-induced variance. Implementing a weighting scheme (e.g., inverse-variance weighting based on the 256 realizations) will significantly sharpen the OoD signal. 
3. **Robust Validation**: Shifting from a Gaussian blur proxy to a "Leave-One-Cosmology-Out" or "Extreme-Nuisance-Proxy" validation will provide a much more rigorous estimate of the model's ability to distinguish between physical structure and nuisance variation. 
These changes are high-ROI: they leverage the existing infrastructure while directly addressing the "Reviewer Confidence" and "Structural Robustness" criteria, moving the project toward a scientifically rigorous publication.
